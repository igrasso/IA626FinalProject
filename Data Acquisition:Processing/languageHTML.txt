<p><a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> is the third-most populous <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">state</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. Its residents include people from a wide variety of <a href="/wiki/Ethnic" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic">ethnic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Race_(classification_of_human_beings)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (classification of human beings)">racial</a>, national and <a href="/wiki/Religious" class="mw-redirect" title="Religious">religious</a> backgrounds.  The state has attracted immigrants, particularly from Latin America.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Florida's majority <a href="/wiki/Ethnic" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic">ethnic</a> group are <a href="/wiki/European_Americans" title="European Americans">European Americans</a>, with approximately 65% of the population identifying as <a href="/wiki/White_people" title="White people">White</a>. National ethnic communities in the state include <a href="/wiki/Cubans" title="Cubans">Cubans</a>, who migrated <i>en masse</i> following the revolution in mid-century. They have been joined by other immigrants from Latin America, and Spanish is spoken by more than 20% of the state's population, with high usage especially in the <a href="/wiki/Miami-Dade_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami-Dade County">Miami-Dade County</a> area.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With a population of 18.8 million according to the 2010 census, Florida is the most populous state in the <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_United_States" title="Southeastern United States">Southeastern United States</a>, and the second-most populous state in the South behind <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>. Within the United States, it contains the highest percentage of people over 65 (17.3%), and the 8th fewest people under 18 (21.9%).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Race.2FEthnicity"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Race/Ethnicity">Race/Ethnicity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race/Ethnicity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">2010 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the 2010 census, the racial distributions are as follows; 77.3% <a href="/wiki/White_people" title="White people">White</a> (53.5% Non-Hispanic White), 16.9% <a href="/wiki/African_American" class="mw-redirect" title="African American">African American</a> (includes Afro-Caribbeans), 2.9% <a href="/wiki/Asian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian American">Asian American</a>, and 0.5% <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a>. 25.6% of the population are <a href="/wiki/Hispanics" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanics">Hispanics</a> or Latino (of any race). Florida has one of the largest African-American populations in the country, and has the second-highest Latino population on the East Coast outside of <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> state. Its ethnic Asian population has grown rapidly since the late 1990s; the majority are <a href="/wiki/South_Asian_Americans" title="South Asian Americans">South Asians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Filipino_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Filipino American">Filipinos</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_Americans" title="Vietnamese Americans">Vietnamese</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Americans" title="Chinese Americans">ethnic Chinese</a>. The state has some federally recognized American Indian tribes, such as the <a href="/wiki/Seminole" title="Seminole">Seminoles</a> in the southeastern part of the state.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>According to the 2018 US Census Bureau estimates, Florida's population was 74.7% <a href="/wiki/White_American" class="mw-redirect" title="White American">White</a> (53.3% Non-Hispanic White), 16.0% Black or <a href="/wiki/African_American" class="mw-redirect" title="African American">African American</a>, 2.8% <a href="/wiki/Asian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian American">Asian</a>, 0.3% <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alaskan_Native" class="mw-redirect" title="Alaskan Native">Alaskan Native</a>, 0.1% <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander" title="Pacific Islander">Pacific Islander</a>, 3.3% Some Other Race, and 2.9% from <a href="/wiki/Multiracial_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Multiracial American">two or more races</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CensusACS2018_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CensusACS2018-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The White population continues to remain the largest racial category as Hispanics in Florida primarily identify as White (81.9%) with others identifying as Some Other Race (11.3%), Multiracial (3.4%), Black (2.8%), American Indian and Alaskan Native (0.3%), Asian (0.1%), and Hawaiian and Pacific Islander (0.1%).<sup id="cite_ref-CensusACS2018_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CensusACS2018-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> By ethnicity, 26.1% of the total population is <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_and_Latino_Americans" title="Hispanic and Latino Americans">Hispanic-Latino</a> (of any race) and 73.9% is Non-Hispanic (of any race). If treated as a separate category, Hispanics are the largest minority group in group in Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-CensusACS2018_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CensusACS2018-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As in other Deep South states settled largely in the 19th century, the vast majority have British Isles ancestry.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br />
</p>

<p>As of 2010<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Florida&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, 73.36% of Florida residents age 5 and older spoke <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> at home as a <a href="/wiki/Primary_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Primary language">primary language</a>, while 19.54% spoke <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, 1.84% <a href="/wiki/French-based_creole_languages" title="French-based creole languages">French Creole</a> (mostly <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Creole" title="Haitian Creole">Haitian Creole</a>), 0.60% <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> and 0.50% <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>. In total, 26.64% of Florida's population age 5 and older spoke a <a href="/wiki/Mother_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Mother language">mother language</a> other than English.<sup id="cite_ref-MLA_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MLA-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Florida's <a href="/wiki/Public_education" class="mw-redirect" title="Public education">public education</a> system identified more than 200 first languages other than English spoken in the homes of students.<sup id="cite_ref-MacDonald_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MacDonald-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 1990, the <a href="/wiki/League_of_United_Latin_American_Citizens" title="League of United Latin American Citizens">League of United Latin American Citizens (LULAC)</a> settled a <a href="/wiki/Class_action_lawsuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Class action lawsuit">class action lawsuit</a> against the state <a href="/wiki/Florida_Department_of_Education" title="Florida Department of Education">Florida Department of Education</a> with a consent decree that required educators to be trained in teaching <a href="/wiki/English_language_learning_and_teaching" class="mw-redirect" title="English language learning and teaching">English for Speakers of Other Languages</a> (ESOL).<sup id="cite_ref-consentdecree_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-consentdecree-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Florida_Constitution#Article_II:_&quot;General_Provisions&quot;" class="mw-redirect" title="Florida Constitution">Article II, Section 9</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="Florida Constitution">Florida Constitution</a> provides that "English is the <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official language</a> of the State of Florida." This provision was adopted in 1988 by a vote following an Initiative <a href="/wiki/Petition" title="Petition">Petition</a>.
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/North_American_English_regional_phonology#Miami_accent" title="North American English regional phonology">Miami accent</a> has developed among persons born and/or raised in and around <a href="/wiki/Miami-Dade_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami-Dade County">Miami-Dade County</a> and a few other parts of <a href="/wiki/Miami_metropolitan_area" title="Miami metropolitan area">South Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> It is more prominent among <a href="/wiki/Hispanics" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanics">Hispanics</a> (especially <a href="/wiki/Cuban_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Cuban American">Cuban Americans</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_and_Latino_Americans" title="Hispanic and Latino Americans">Latino</a> groups, influenced by the Spanish language).<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Veterans">Veterans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Veterans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There were 1.6 million veterans in Florida in 2010, representing 8% of the total population.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Migration">Migration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Migration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013, most net migrants come from 1) <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, 2) <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, 3) <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, and 4) the <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_United_States" title="Midwestern United States">Midwestern United States</a>; emigration is higher from these same states. For example, about 50,000 moved to New York; but more than 50,000 people moved from New York to Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
